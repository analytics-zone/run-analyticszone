About RunAnalytics

Science-based running performance tracking, built by runners for runners

Our Mission

RunAnalytics brings professional-grade performance tracking to every runner. We believe that advanced metrics like Critical Run Speed (CRS), Training Stress Score (TSS), and Performance Management Charts shouldn't be locked behind expensive platforms or require complex coaching software.

Scientific Foundation

RunAnalytics is built on decades of peer-reviewed sports science research:

Critical Run Speed (CRS)

Based on Wakayoshi et al. (1992-1993) research at Osaka University. CRS represents the theoretical maximum running velocity sustainable without exhaustion, corresponding to lactate threshold.

Key Research: Wakayoshi K, et al. "Determination and validity of critical velocity as an index of running performance." European Journal of Applied Physiology, 1992.

Training Stress Score (TSS)

Adapted from Dr. Andrew Coggan's cycling TSS methodology for running. Quantifies training load by combining intensity (relative to CRS) and duration.

Key Research: Coggan AR, Allen H. "Training and Racing with a Power Meter." VeloPress, 2010. Adapted for running by RunAnalytics using CRS as threshold.

Performance Management Chart (PMC)

Chronic Training Load (CTL), Acute Training Load (ATL), and Training Stress Balance (TSB) metrics. Tracks fitness, fatigue, and form over time.

Implementation: 42-day exponentially weighted moving average for CTL, 7-day for ATL. TSB = CTL - ATL.

Running Efficiency & Stride Metrics

Running efficiency metrics combining time and stride count. Used by elite runners and coaches worldwide to track technical improvements.

Standard Metrics: Running Efficiency = Time + Strides. Lower scores indicate better efficiency. Supplemented by Distance Per Stride (DPS) and Stride Rate (SR).
